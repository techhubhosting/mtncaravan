Day One: First Destination Journey

The Journey begins from either Kampala City or Entebbe airport to either Kibale (chimpanzees) or Bwindi (Gorillas) depending on your first destination choice and may take between 7-9 hours.
You will be provided with meals and accommodation on arrival as we rest and freshen up for the next day.

Day Two: Incase Gorillas First

The day is gone; you are now in the southwestern part of Uganda, waking up to a cool morning, Early breakfast already served on the table. Warm yourself up for the day of your memories. From breakfast you will be driven to Rhiija park headquarters for a briefing on the whole exercise. This will end in the trek into the jungles. Depending on the location of the families of these Gorillas, the journey can take you from 1-6 hours. Once they are found, you will be given one hour to spend with them observing their manners, this as stipulated by the Wild Life Authority. In this hour you can take photos, and any other friendly interactions and from this, w shall return to the headquarters where you will get the certificate as confirmation that you have successfully conducted this gorilla tour. From this, we return to the hotel for an overnight stay.

Day Four: Chimpanzee Trekking, Kibale and Bigodi jungles.

After breakfast, we drive through to the headquarters of Kibale national park for a short brief on the approach to this event. Here if groups are big, they are split into teams of six each headed by an experienced guide who will take them through the forests to find some families of these Primates. Interesting to note is that such families can consist of between 40-160 individuals which usually fragment into smaller groups during the day as they hunt for food to reduce competition for a meal. They usually come back together in the evening to arrange for their night. Once these are located, the guides will usually give you about an hour to interact around them, before returning to the base for Lunch. After lunch with more time to spare, we move to the Bigodi wetlands where you get a chance to view more than 10 primate species, lots of birds, etc, this is definitely your best day perhaps.
